# The Coca-Cola Company — Business Operations KPI Analysis

1. Key Financial Metrics

Net Income Volatility (FY 2022):
Overall performance weakened in 2022 mainly because of a $262 million net non-operating loss. This was caused by:
$371 million loss on equity and debt investments
$236 million loss from foreign currency movements
$96 million impairment related to Russian operations
These were partly offset by:
$219 million pension benefit income
$153 million gain from refranchising Cambodia bottling operations

Reporting Boundary Changes:
Data for FY 2023 and FY 2024 includes changes in reporting structure due to acquisitions and refranchising, which affects year-over-year comparison.

Operating Profit Pressure (FY 2024):
EBIT and EBITDA declined mainly due to $4.16 billion in Other Operating Charges, including:
$3.1 billion related to the Fairlife acquisition (liability remeasurement)
$760 million impairment of the BodyArmor brand

2. Profitability and Expense Analysis

Gross Margin Improvement:
Gross margin increased to 61.1% in 2024 from 59.5% in 2023. This was mainly due to better pricing and refranchising in Asia, which helped offset higher commodity costs and currency impact.

Net Income vs EBIT:
In FY 2024, Net Income was higher than EBIT because of one-time non-operating gains, not because of stronger core operations.

Operating Expenses:
SG&A expenses increased by 4% ($610 million) due to higher advertising and stock-based compensation. This was partly offset by lower distribution costs.

Refranchising Gains:
The company recorded gains from selling bottling operations:
$595 million (Philippines)
$506 million (Thailand)
$338 million (Coke Consolidated)
$303 million (India)

3. Segment and Geographic Performance

Business Segments:
The company operates through Concentrate and Finished Product segments. The Concentrate segment is currently the main growth driver.

Geographic Performance:
North America remains the largest revenue contributor. International regions were affected by foreign currency changes, which impacted reported growth even when underlying demand was stable.

4. Unit Case Volume Trends

Global Volume:
Total system volume increased slightly to 33.7 billion unit cases in 2024.

Regional Performance:
EMEA: Flat growth (0%). Growth in water and sports drinks was offset by declines in juice and Trademark Coke.
Latin America: +3% growth, driven by Brazil (+8%) and Mexico (+2%), despite a sharp decline in Argentina (-12%).
North America: Flat growth (0%). Growth in juice and dairy was offset by a 4% decline in water and tea.
Asia Pacific: +1% growth, supported by India (+7%) and ASEAN markets, offset by a 5% decline in China.

Strategic Changes:
Global Ventures grew by 2% (mainly energy drinks), while Bottling Investments fell by 23% due to continued refranchising of bottling operations.

5. Liquidity, Solvency, and Efficiency

Debt Levels:
Debt ratios remained stable over the years, showing balanced growth in assets and liabilities.

Interest Coverage (FY 2022):
Interest coverage increased in 2022 mainly because interest expense fell to $882 million. This was due to one-time charges of $650 million in 2021 related to debt repayment and hedging activities. Higher interest rates in 2022 partly reduced this benefit.

Liquidity Ratios:
Current, quick, and cash ratios declined mainly because current liabilities grew faster than current assets.

Working Capital Efficiency:
Despite lower liquidity ratios, efficiency improved as seen in:
Lower DSO and DIO
Higher DPO
Much shorter Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)

Receivables Factoring:

The company sold trade receivables to financial institutions to receive cash faster. It sold:
$21,873 million in 2024
$17,704 million in 2022
This reduced reported receivables, improved cash flow, and lowered liquidity ratios.

Overall View:
These trends show better cash management and efficiency, not liquidity problems.

6. ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)

Emissions:
Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions stayed stable. The large increase in Scope 3 emissions was mainly due to reporting changes from acquisitions and refranchising, not operational issues.

Energy:
Total energy use dropped sharply while renewable energy use increased significantly. The report does not clearly explain the exact reason for this change.

Water Management:
Water efficiency improved. Even though total water use increased, water used per unit of revenue decreased, meaning the company generated more value using less water per revenue.
